Formula 1 or short F1, is a racingsport with 10 different teams. Each team has 2 designated drivers.
The sports exist since 1950, in 1981 it became the Fia Formula One World Championship.
During a World Championship, the teams has around 21 races before it ends.

Raceweekend

Each race is spread over a entire weekend.

  • Friday

    On friday there are two of the three free practice sessions. These sessions last 1 hour and 30 minutes and are usefull for the teams to get a feeling of the circuit and adjust the car to each circuit.

  • Saturday

    This day, the last free practice finds place and the qualifying. Qualifying is a knockout system in three stages. The entire qualifying has a duration of 1 hour and determine the grid for the race on sunday.
    In the first part, the drivers have 18 minutes to get in the top 15 based on their lap time. After a break the 15 remaining drivers try to get in the top 10. The last session is 12 minutes long and determine the final top 10.

  • Sunday
    Sunday is raceday. The amount of laps differs from circuit to circuit and is the time where points can be scored for the championship.

Point system

Points are awarded to the team and the driver. At the end of the championship you have the best driver and the best team.
At the end of the race, the first 10 drivers get points in the following order: 25, 18, 15, 12, 10, 8, 6, 4, 2 and 1.
The driver with the fastet lap and a position in the top 10 gets a extra point.
